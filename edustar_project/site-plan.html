Section 01

Site Name

⚡ EduStar

EduStar fuses Education and Star — a name that communicates academic aspiration and positions learners as the stars of their own journey. The word is short, memorable, and works equally well in English across all 10+ target countries on the continent. The star symbol (⭐) doubles as a universally understood mark of excellence and achievement, making it ideal for a learning platform aimed at students striving toward national examinations.

🌐 Suggested domain: edustar.africa

Section 02

Site Purpose

EduStar is a free, AI-powered learning platform for secondary school students (Grades 8–12) across sub-Saharan Africa. Millions of students prepare annually for high-stakes national examinations — ZIMSEC, WAEC, KCSE, NECTA, ECZ, CAPS, and more — often without access to reliable textbooks or structured study support.

The site serves as a centralised hub offering three core services:

📚 Free Textbook Library

A dynamically loaded digital library of 30+ open-access textbooks sourced from Siyavula, OpenStax, and Maktaba — filterable by country, grade, and subject. Students can view details and open books directly in their browser at no cost.

🤖 AI-Powered Learning

Curriculum-aligned lessons, adaptive quizzes, and study explanations generated with AI, personalised to the student's country, grade, and subject choices.

💬 Peer Community & Registration

A registration system allowing students to create a free account and join a cross-African peer community where they can ask questions, share notes, and support one another.

Section 03

Scenarios

The following questions represent the kinds of things a target site visitor — a secondary school student in Africa — might ask when arriving on EduStar for the first time.

  • I am sitting ZIMSEC O-Level Mathematics next term — where can I find a free textbook that matches my syllabus?
  • I live in Zambia and study under the ECZ curriculum. Does EduStar have resources specifically for my grade and country?
  • I don't have a tutor. Can I use EduStar to get explanations of topics I don't understand in Physics?
  • How do I create a free account so I can save my progress and connect with other students across Africa?
  • Can I filter the book library to show only Grade 10 Biology books available for Nigeria (WAEC)?

Section 04

Color Scheme

The palette draws on the energy of the African sun (orange/gold) and the cool clarity of technology (teal/blue). A deep navy background reduces eye strain during late-night study sessions — a practical choice for the target audience.

Background #0f1117

Page background — deep navy. Reduces eye strain for extended study sessions.

Surface / Card #1e2535

Cards, panels, and form fields — slightly lighter than BG to create depth.

Sun Orange — Primary #ff6b2b

Primary buttons, CTAs, active nav links, key headings and gradient accents.

Gold — Secondary #ffb347

Gradient partner to orange; used in headline gradients and card headings.

Teal — Accent #00c9a7

Section labels, border accents, badges, and hover states.

Text #e8edf5

Primary body text — off-white for softer contrast against dark background.

Muted Text #8a96aa

Subtitles, metadata, placeholder text, and secondary descriptions.

Border #2a3347

Card borders, dividers, input outlines — subtle separation without harsh lines.

Section 05

Typography

Two Google Fonts are selected — one high-impact display face for headings and one highly legible sans-serif for body copy. Both are served via Google Fonts with a preconnect hint for performance.

Font 1 — Display / Headings

Syne — Quality Education, Powered by AI

Family: Syne  ·  Weights used: 700, 800  ·  Source: Google Fonts (OFL)

Used for: Page headings (h1–h3), navigation logo, section titles, button labels, stat numbers, and any large display text requiring strong visual impact.

Why Syne: Its wide, geometric uppercase letterforms convey confidence and modernity — positioning EduStar as a forward-looking platform, not a traditional textbook publisher.

Font 2 — Body / UI

DM Sans — Access curriculum-aligned lessons, free textbooks, and a peer-learning community tailored to your country, grade, and pace.

Family: DM Sans  ·  Weights used: 300, 400, 500, 600  ·  Source: Google Fonts (OFL)

Used for: Body paragraphs, form labels, input fields, card descriptions, footer text, badges, and all UI copy at smaller sizes.

Why DM Sans: Designed for digital interfaces, DM Sans is highly legible at both large and small sizes. Its neutral character pairs cleanly with Syne without competing for attention.

📐 Type Scale

Display (h1) — Syne 800 · clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem) · Headings and hero titles

Heading (h2) — Syne 800 · clamp(1.4rem, 3vw, 2rem) · Section titles

Subheading (h3) — Syne 700 · 1rem–1.1rem · Card headings

Body — DM Sans 400 · 1rem · Paragraphs and descriptions

Small / Meta — DM Sans 400–500 · 0.78rem–0.88rem · Labels, badges, captions

Label — DM Sans 700 · 0.72rem · Uppercase section labels (teal, 2px letter-spacing)
